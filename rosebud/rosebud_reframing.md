# Reframing（重新框架）

A variant of the Reframing Negative Thoughts skill with an expanded theoretical emphasis on multiple reframing angles and cultural sensitivity.

## Relationship to Reframing Negative Thoughts

Both skills use the same 5-section CBT reframing structure (Identify → Understand → Challenge → Reframe → Reflect). This version includes additional depth in:
- Evidence examination methodology (courtroom analogy)
- ACT creative hopelessness emphasis
- Cultural adaptation for Chinese/Taiwanese context
- Expanded branching conditions for different response styles

See [rosebud_reframing_negative_thoughts.md](rosebud_reframing_negative_thoughts.md) for the core framework.

## Theoretical Foundations

Same core theories as Reframing Negative Thoughts, with additional emphasis on:

### Evidence-Based Examination
- **Support vs. oppose:** list evidence for and against the belief, see which side is more convincing
- **Reality testing:** turn abstract fears into verifiable predictions, then test with experience
- Example: "If I say no, they'll definitely be angry" → actually test it

### Cultural Adaptation (Chinese Context)
1. **Avoid hyper-individualism:** not "live for yourself" but "care for yourself while caring for others"
2. **Reduce vulnerability shame:** normalize with "many people think this way"; use "learning" frame over "therapy"
3. **Respect collective values:** don't push "defy authority" or "express anger"; explore "within existing constraints, what's possible?"

### Expanded Response Style Adaptations

| Response Style | Approach |
|---------------|----------|
| Very short | Switch to feeling/body cues: "What does your chest or shoulders feel like?" Use multiple choice over open-ended |
| Very long/scattered | Gently focus: "If you could pick the ONE most stuck point, which?" |
| Heavy emotion | Stabilize first, stay in Understanding stage, normalize: "This feeling is completely reasonable" |
| Stuck/no ideas | Provide scaffolding: "Some people feel [X], do you?" or "What would you tell a friend?" |
| Has insight | Amplify and connect to action: "This discovery matters — what would you do differently?" |

## Effect Expectations

- **Short-term (now):** emotional intensity decreases, thought loosens, new angle visible
- **Medium-term (days):** try small actions, more awareness of negative thoughts
- **Long-term (repeated practice):** automatic negative thinking decreases, psychological flexibility increases
- **Not guaranteed:** thoughts completely disappear (unrealistic), one session solves everything, replaces professional treatment

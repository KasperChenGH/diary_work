# Daily Intention（每日意圖）

Morning journaling ritual to shift from reactive mode to intentional design for the day.

## Purpose

- Transform vague morning anxiety into clear intention
- Switch from passive reaction to active design mode
- Build a morning ritual with direction, not pressure

## Core Functions

| Function | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Emotion scan | Quick body/mind state check to set conversation pace |
| Priority clarification | From "20 things to do" to "today's real 1-2 things" |
| Intention setting | Transform goals into "how I want to feel" not just "what to complete" |
| Obstacle rehearsal | Identify triggers for distraction/anxiety, design If-Then responses |
| Continuity building | Connect recent journal entries into a "yesterday → today → tomorrow" narrative |

## Conversation Flow (5 Stages)

### Stage 0: State Scan (1-2 rounds)
- "What time is it? How did you sleep?"
- "Energy level 1-10?"
- "Any notable body sensations?"

### Stage 1: Today's Focus (2-3 rounds)
- "If you could only finish one thing today, what would it be?"
- "What's 'must do' vs. 'want to do'?"
- "What would make you feel 'today wasn't wasted' tonight?"

### Stage 2: Intention Deepening (1-2 rounds)
- "When you finish this, how do you want to feel?"
- "One word to describe the state you want today?"

### Stage 3: Obstacle Anticipation (1-2 rounds, optional)
- "What might distract or trigger anxiety today?"
- "If you want to impulse-buy at noon → ?"
- "If research gets stuck, your Plan B?"

### Stage 4: Closing (1 round)
- "Take this sentence with you today: ___"

## Summary Template

**Your State:** energy level + core emotion
**Today's Priorities:**
1. [most important] (estimated time)
2. [secondary] (estimated time)
3. [if energy allows] (estimated time)

**Desired State:** "[keyword or phrase]"

**Obstacles + Strategies:**
- If [trigger] → Then [response]

**Today's One Line:** "[anchor reminder for when anxious]"

## Theoretical Foundations

### 1. Mindfulness + Intention Setting
- Awareness without judgment; conscious choice over autopilot
- Intention (who to be) > goal (what to do)
- Stage 0: mindful scan; Stage 2: "who do I want to be today"

### 2. CBT — Behavioral Activation
- Action before motivation; small steps lower activation resistance
- Stage 1: break big goals into today's minimum viable version
- Stage 3: If-Then planning reduces cognitive load

### 3. Self-Determination Theory (SDT)
- Intrinsic motivation > external pressure; autonomy, competence, relatedness
- Stage 1: "what makes today not wasted" = connect to core values
- Stage 2: shift from "should" to "want to"

### 4. Implementation Intentions (Gollwitzer)
- If-Then plans are 2-3x more effective than plain goals
- Pre-decisions reduce cognitive load
- Stage 3: design specific If-Then strategies

### 5. Narrative Therapy
- Person ≠ problem; reinterpretation changes meaning
- Stage 0-1: reframe from "I can't finish everything" to "I'm learning to prioritize"
- Stage 4: closing line = new self-narrative to carry

### 6. Positive Psychology
- Focus on strengths, not deficits; gratitude and anticipation boost well-being
- Stage 2: "what experience do you want?" activates positive emotion
- Stage 4: affirmation and blessing

## Branch Conditions

| User State | Approach |
|------------|----------|
| **Short answers (1-2 sentences)** | Don't press for details; switch to yes/no or multiple-choice questions to lower cognitive load |
| **Long answers (3+ minutes of voice)** | Mirror core emotion first, then converge: "It sounds like you care about X and Y -- is that right?" |
| **Heavy emotion (anxiety/sadness/anger)** | Empathize + normalize ("that makes sense"), pause goal-setting, ask "how can you take care of yourself today?" |
| **Calm emotion** | Normal flow; can use challenging questions ("Really?" / "What else?") |
| **Stuck / don't know** | Offer options ("A or B?") or lower difficulty ("What's the smallest step today?") |
| **Insight moment** | Seize it immediately! Follow up: "What does this discovery mean to you?" then help convert to action |

## Full Output Example

> Daily Intention | 2026-03-28 Friday

**Your State:**
Energy: 7/10 (sleep was okay, but some anxiety)
Core emotion: wanting to do a lot, but afraid of not finishing

**Today's Priorities:**
1. Research: check statistics teacher's survey collection progress (15 min)
2. English: complete one Writing Task 2 practice (30 min)
3. Work: draft midterm exam question section 2 (45 min)

**Desired State:**
"Steady progress, not chasing perfection"

**Obstacles + Strategies:**
- If I want to impulse-buy at noon → write it on the list, decide after 72 hours
- If research gets stuck → switch to English practice, come back tonight
- If anxiety spikes → 5-minute breathing + look at balcony plants

**Today's One Line:**
"You don't have to run the whole race today -- just don't stop."

## Change Mechanisms

1. **Reactive → Design mode:** choose today's rhythm instead of being chased by to-do lists
2. **Perfectionism → Good enough:** from "finish everything" to "do the 1-2 most important things well"
3. **External → Internal drive:** from "I should" to "I want to"
4. **Isolated action → Continuous narrative:** each day is part of "who you're becoming"
5. **Anxiety-driven → Intention-driven:** morning shifts from dread to direction

# Boundary Setting（邊界設定）

Guided exploration to move from "I know I should set boundaries but can't" to actually executing a boundary strategy.

## Purpose

Help you recognize boundary violations, understand your patterns, and design a practical boundary script you can use.

## Exploration Directions (Choose 1+)

1. **Setting boundaries at work** — handling requests, overtime, extra tasks
2. **Setting boundaries in personal relationships** — family, friends, partner expectations
3. **Learning to say no** — practicing specific language and mental preparation
4. **Dealing with boundary violations** — responding when boundaries are already crossed

## Challenge Types

- Difficulty saying no
- Worrying about disappointing others
- Feeling selfish for having boundaries
- Struggling to maintain boundaries once set

## Conversation Flow (8 Stages)

### Stage 0: Choose Direction
- "你想探索哪個領域的邊界？（工作/人際/學習說不/處理侵犯）"
- "這些選項中，哪個讓你最有感覺？"

### Stage 1: Identify Challenges — specify what makes it hard
- "在設邊界時，你最常遇到什麼困難？"
- "是「說不」本身很難，還是「說了之後的後果」讓你擔心？"
- "你覺得自己是「不知道怎麼說」，還是「知道但做不到」？"

### Stage 2: Dig into a Specific Event — find a real anchor situation
- "最近有哪一次「你想說不，但最後還是答應了」的請求？"
- "當時是誰找你？要你做什麼？"
- "你當下的感受是什麼？（煩躁、內疚、無奈、憤怒？）"
- "你答應的那一刻，腦中閃過什麼念頭？"

### Stage 3: Unpack Inner Dynamics — why you agreed, what you're protecting, what you fear
- "如果你當時說不，你最擔心會發生什麼？"
- "你覺得對方會怎麼看你？"
- "你在保護什麼？（關係、形象、和諧、避免衝突？）"
- "這個擔心有多真實？過去有實際發生過嗎？"

### Stage 4: Explore Patterns & Beliefs — recurring patterns, core beliefs ("If I say no, it means...")
- "這種「想拒絕但答應」的情況，是不是常常發生？"
- "你有沒有發現一個模式：什麼樣的人、什麼樣的請求，你特別難拒絕？"
- "你心中有沒有一個聲音說「我應該⋯⋯」或「如果我不⋯⋯就⋯⋯」？"
- "如果你的好朋友遇到同樣情況，你會怎麼建議他？"

### Stage 5: Reframe & Choose — challenge old beliefs, offer new perspectives
- "如果「說不」不代表你自私，而是「保護你的優先順序」，感覺有什麼不同？"
- "你有沒有想過：對方也可以承受你的拒絕？"
- "你答應這件事，代價是什麼？（時間、精力、你真正想做的事）"
- "你願意為了避免讓對方失望，付出多少代價？"

### Stage 6: Design Boundary Strategy — co-create a script you can actually say
- "如果可以重來，你想怎麼回應那個請求？"
- "你需要一個「緩衝句」嗎？（例如：「讓我看一下行程再回覆你」）"
- "你可以說什麼，既表達拒絕，又不會讓你覺得太內疚？"
- "我們一起寫一個你能說出口的版本，好嗎？"

### Stage 7: Rehearse & Prepare — imagine resistance, prepare Plan B
- "如果你真的這樣說，對方可能會怎麼反應？"
- "你準備好面對對方的失望或不滿嗎？"
- "如果對方繼續施壓，你的 Plan B 是什麼？"
- "下次遇到類似情況，你想提醒自己什麼？"

## Summary Template

**Core Challenge:** [situation where boundaries are hardest]
**Your Pattern:** [recurring behavior pattern]
**Core Belief:** [the "should" or "if...then..." driving it]
**Reframe:** [new perspective]
**Boundary Script:** [exact words you can say]
**Next Step Action:** [concrete, executable small step]
**If-Then Strategies:** 2 contingency plans

## Branch Conditions

| User State | Adjustment |
|-----------|------------|
| Short answers ("don't know", "it's just hard") | Provide specific options or scenarios: "Is it one of these: A/B/C?" or "Let's start from the last time it happened" |
| Long answers / heavy emotion | Mirror feelings first, then focus: "Sounds like you were really torn/angry/helpless. Among all these feelings, which one bothers you most?" |
| Calm emotion | Move faster through exploration, go deeper into patterns and beliefs |
| Stuck / doesn't know how to answer | Lower difficulty or provide examples: "Let's try a different angle — if your friend were in this situation, how would you see it?" |
| Has insight ("I realize I always...") | Deepen and connect to action: "That's an important discovery. What do you think you could do differently next time?" |

## Full Output Example

**Core Challenge:**
You find it hard to refuse extra tasks from your boss at work because you worry "saying no will make me look not dedicated enough" and fear it will affect your promotion.

**Your Pattern:**
You find it especially hard to say no to authority figures. Growing up you were taught to "be obedient and cooperative." You use "agree first, handle it yourself later" to avoid conflict, at the cost of your own time and health.

**Core Belief:**
"If I say no, it means I'm not hardworking enough / not good enough."

**Reframe:**
Saying no is not rejecting the other person — it's protecting your priorities. You can be a good employee while also guarding your boundaries.

**Boundary Script:**
"謝謝你想到我。我手上有幾個優先項目，如果接這個任務，可能會影響 X 的進度。我們可以討論一下優先順序嗎？"

**Next Step Action:**
Next time your boss proposes extra work, say "讓我確認一下手上的工作量，明天早上回覆你" — give yourself buffer time.

**If-Then Strategies:**
- If the other person pressures you -> repeat your boundary, don't over-explain
- If you start feeling guilty -> remind yourself: "I'm protecting my priorities, not rejecting the person"

## Theoretical Foundations

### 1. CBT
- Thought → emotion → behavior cycle; core beliefs affect automatic thoughts
- Stages 3-4: uncover "If I say no, then..." automatic thoughts
- Stage 5: challenge beliefs; Stage 6: behavioral experiment
- **Change mechanism:** When you see "I must agree" is a belief, not a fact, you gain space to choose new behavior.

### 2. DBT — Interpersonal Effectiveness (DEAR MAN)
- Describe, Express, Assert, Reinforce, Mindful, Appear confident, Negotiate
- Stage 6: structure boundary scripts using DEAR MAN
- Stage 7: rehearse maintaining relationship while refusing
- **Change mechanism:** You learn that "saying no" does not equal "destroying the relationship" — it means "honestly expressing your needs."

### 3. Attachment Theory
- Early attachment shapes adult boundary patterns
- Anxious attachment → over-compliance to maintain connection
- Stage 4: exploring "what are you protecting" often touches attachment
- **Change mechanism:** When you understand "I'm afraid to say no because I fear being abandoned/unloved," you can start practicing the new experience of "even if I say no, the relationship is still safe."

### 4. ACT
- Values-based action; cognitive defusion; committed action despite discomfort
- "You can carry guilt and still say no"
- **Change mechanism:** You don't need to wait until "I don't feel guilty" to say no; you can carry the guilt and still choose values-aligned action.

### 5. Narrative Therapy
- Problem externalization; rewriting identity stories; unique outcomes
- From "I'm someone who can't say no" to "I'm learning to protect my priorities"
- **Change mechanism:** When you redefine "I can't say no" from "my nature" to "a pattern I'm changing," you gain space for action.

### 6. Motivational Interviewing
- Explore ambivalence; elicit change talk; respect autonomy
- "How much cost are you willing to pay to avoid disappointing them?"
- **Change mechanism:** When you say "I want to change" yourself, rather than being told "you should change," your commitment is much stronger.

### 7. Trauma-Informed Approach
- Boundary difficulty may stem from relational trauma
- Safety first; empowerment; start from the smallest version you can manage
- **Change mechanism:** When you feel safe and respected, you have the space to truly explore and change.

## Change Mechanisms

1. Concrete → Abstract → Concrete: real event → patterns/beliefs → reframe → actionable script
2. Understanding → Action: not just "know why" but "design how"
3. Respect your pace: choose exploration depth, can stop anytime
4. Small steps, executable: boundary script must be "something you can try tomorrow"

---

Change is not one-shot -- it's cumulative small practice. (改變不是一次到位，而是一次次小練習的累積。)

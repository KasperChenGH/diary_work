# Dream Journal（夢境日記）

Guided dream exploration that helps you move from "remembering a dream" to "understanding its meaning" and applying insights to real life.

## Purpose

Help you understand dreams as expressions of inner wisdom, connecting dream symbols and emotions to your waking life, and turning insights into actionable directions.

## Core Functions

- **Guided dream exploration** — questions to help recall and describe dream details
- **Symbol & metaphor interpretation** — discover personal meaning of dream symbols
- **Emotional connection** — link dream feelings to real-life experiences
- **Insight integration** — find actionable directions from dream understanding

## Conversation Flow

### Stage 0: Opening Invitation
- "Can you describe your dream with as much detail as possible?"

### Stage 1: Dream Description & Detail Exploration
> **Gestalt technique:** Encourage describing in present tense, as if re-experiencing the dream right now.

- "What did the scene look like? Any notable details?"
- "Who or what appeared in the dream? What were they doing?"
- "What did you feel in the dream? Fear, excitement, confusion?"
- "Was the atmosphere bright or dark? Fast-paced or slow?"
- "What stood out most or left the strongest impression?"

### Stage 2: Symbol & Metaphor Analysis
- "What does [symbol] mean to you personally?"
- "Does this [person/object] have special significance in your life?"
- "If this dream were a metaphor, what would it be saying?"
- "What do these symbols remind you of in your life?"

### Stage 3: Emotion & Reality Connection
- "Have you felt something similar to this dream emotion recently in real life?"
- "What current life situation does this dream remind you of?"
- "How does the dream emotion relate to your current state?"

### Stage 4: Insight & Application
- "What inspiration or reminder does this dream give you?"
- "If this dream is a message from your subconscious, what is it telling you?"
- "What did you learn about yourself from this dream?"

### Stage 5: Closing Summary
- "還有什麼想補充的嗎？"
- Then provide structured summary

## Branch Conditions

| User State | Adjustment |
|-----------|------------|
| Short answers | More specific follow-ups with concrete details |
| Long answers | Affirm richness, then focus on one key element |
| Heavy emotion | Empathize first, slow down, don't rush to analysis |
| Calm emotion | Move faster to symbol analysis and insights |
| Stuck | Provide options or reframe: "If this dream were a movie, what would its theme be?" |
| Has insight | Immediately affirm and deepen |

## Theoretical Foundations

### 1. Jungian Analytical Psychology
- **Personal & collective unconscious:** dreams are the language of the unconscious
- **Symbols & archetypes:** dream symbols have personal meaning and may connect to universal human experience
- **Compensation function:** dreams compensate for what consciousness ignores
- Applies to: Stage 2 (symbol analysis), Stage 3 (emotional connection)
- **Why it helps:** lets you understand dreams as expressions of inner wisdom, helping you access needs or conflicts that consciousness normally misses

### 2. Gestalt Therapy
- **Here and now:** focus on present experience
- **Awareness:** change comes through awareness
- **Every dream element is part of self:** all dream characters represent aspects of you
- Applies to: Stage 1 (present-tense description), Stage 3 (inner voices dialogue)
- **Why it helps:** helps integrate conflicting inner parts (e.g. "the self that wants rest" vs. "the self that thinks resting is wrong"), seeing these internal dialogues through dreams

### 3. CBT-Informed Approach
- **Thought-emotion-behavior link:** dreams reflect cognitive patterns
- **Functional analysis:** understanding the dream's "function"
- **Action-oriented:** from insight to concrete change
- Applies to: Stage 3 (life pattern mapping), Stage 4 (actionable directions)
- **Why it helps:** avoids over-interpretation or abstraction; transforms dream understanding into real-life adjustments (e.g. "this dream reminds me to set boundaries" -> "I can practice saying no at work")

### 4. Narrative Therapy
- **Person is not the problem:** dreams are stories, not diagnoses
- **Power of reinterpretation:** you choose how to understand your dream
- **Externalization:** treat the dream as a dialogue partner
- Applies to: Stage 2 (become your own dream interpreter), Stage 4 (empowering story version)
- **Why it helps:** shifts you from "I had an anxious dream so I'm anxious" to "my dream is reminding me to pay attention to something, and I can choose how to respond" — enhancing sense of agency

## Full Output Example

**Dream Theme:** Lost in an unknown city

**Core Symbols:**
- Unknown city -> represents a new life stage or unfamiliar territory
- Being lost -> reflects anxiety about lack of direction
- Continuously searching for an exit -> desire to find a solution

**Emotional Connection:**
The anxiety in the dream mirrors your current uncertainty about your doctoral thesis direction. You mentioned often feeling "I don't know what I'm doing" — this dream may be a visual representation of that feeling.

**Key Insight:**
This dream reminds you that being lost doesn't mean failure — it's part of the exploration. You may need to allow yourself to "not know" rather than forcing yourself to find an answer immediately.

**Possible Action Directions:**
- Discuss your confusion with your advisor instead of worrying alone
- View "exploration" itself as a valuable process
- Give yourself more time and space to clarify direction

## Change Mechanisms

1. **Vague → Clear:** organize chaotic dreams into meaningful narratives
2. **Passive → Active:** from "dreams happen to me" to "I can learn from dreams"
3. **Isolated → Integrated:** connect dreams to real life
4. **Anxiety → Insight:** transform disturbance into self-understanding

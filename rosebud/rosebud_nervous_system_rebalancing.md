# Nervous System Rebalancing（神經系統再平衡日記）

Identify autonomic nervous system (ANS) triggers and develop personalized coping strategies.

## Purpose

Help you identify a specific ANS trigger, understand your body's response patterns, and find practical, individualized coping strategies — particularly relevant for ME/CFS, Long COVID, and chronic conditions.

## Entry Paths

- **I'm ready to explore my triggers** — directly enter detective work
- **I'd like to calm my nervous system first** — guided breathing/body awareness exercise before exploring

## Conversation Flow (6 Stages)

### Stage 0: Assess Readiness
- "Do you feel ready to explore triggers, or would you like a calming exercise first?"
- "How calm do you feel right now (1-10)?"

### Stage 1: Identify Specific Triggers (core stage)
- "Describe a recent moment when symptoms suddenly changed. What were you doing?"
- "What specific symptoms appeared? How long did they last?"
- "What happened in the hours or day before? (even if seemingly unrelated)"
- "What were you thinking or feeling at the time?"

### Stage 2: Normalize & Reassure
- "Having triggers is completely normal"
- "Being willing to explore them is already a brave step"

### Stage 3: Understand Existing Strategies
- "When this trigger appears, how do you usually cope?"
- "Are these methods helpful? Can you actually use them in the moment?"

### Stage 4: Explore New Coping Strategies
- One suggestion at a time, customized to your situation
- "How does this method sound to you?"
- "What difficulties might you face using it? Confidence level (1-10)?"

### Stage 5: Natural Closing — summary when clarity or completeness is expressed

## Summary Template

**Identified Trigger:** [specific trigger description]
**Your Insight:** [key discovery from the conversation]
**Existing Strategies:** [what you already use + effectiveness]
**New Coping Strategies:** [1-3 specific suggestions, each confirmed by user]
**Confidence Levels:** [score for each strategy]
**Next Step:** [one concrete, executable small action for this week]

## Theoretical Foundations

### 1. Polyvagal Theory (Stephen Porges)
- **Three ANS levels:** social engagement (ventral vagal), fight/flight (sympathetic), shutdown (dorsal vagal)
- **Safety is key:** only in safe state can you effectively learn and integrate
- Stage 0: ensure social engagement state before exploration
- Calming exercises activate parasympathetic system
- **Why it helps:** When your nervous system feels safe, your prefrontal cortex (rational thinking, problem-solving) can function effectively. Exploring triggers while in fight/flight state may cause re-triggering, leading to secondary trauma.

### 2. Trauma-Informed Care
- **Emphasize choice and control:** asking readiness, not diving in
- **Avoid re-traumatization:** one question at a time, prevent overwhelm
- **Respect agency:** "even if it seems unrelated" reduces self-censorship
- **Why it helps:** ME/CFS, Long COVID and other chronic illness patients often have medical trauma (being doubted, dismissed). Providing control and respect rebuilds trust and safety.

### 3. CBT — Functional Analysis
- **Antecedent → Behavior → Consequence:** identify the ABC pattern
- Stage 1: detective-style questioning about what preceded symptoms
- Seeing A → B → C pattern reveals intervention points
- **Why it helps:** When you can see the A (antecedent) → B (behavior/symptom) → C (consequence) pattern clearly, you gain intervention points. You can change A (avoid triggers) or insert a new coping strategy between A and B.

### 4. Motivational Interviewing
- **Change motivation comes from within:** ask "how does this sound?" not "you should do this"
- **Reflective listening:** "It sounds like you..."
- Stage 3-4: explore satisfaction with current methods, confirm resonance before continuing
- **Why it helps:** When you say "I want to change" yourself, change actually happens. If told "you should do this," your brain automatically resists. Through questions and reflection, you find your own answers — answers that feel real and feasible to you.

### 5. Mind-Body Medicine & Chronic Illness Self-Management
- **You are the expert on your body:** strategies must be personalized
- **Small, sustainable changes > big goals**
- Stage 4: one suggestion at a time, customized to real life context
- **Why it helps:** Chronic conditions like ME/CFS are highly individualized — there is no one-size-fits-all solution. What you need is a process that helps you "become the expert on your own body," not a set of standard answers. When strategies grow from your own life context, you can actually follow through.

### 6. Neuroplasticity & Habit Formation
- **New neural pathways through repeated practice**
- **Specific small actions > vague big goals**
- Confidence-weighted strategies: high-confidence items more likely to succeed and reinforce
- **Why it helps:** Your nervous system is already habituated to certain reaction patterns (e.g., stress → push through → crash). Changing this requires small, repeated new actions to gradually build new neural pathways (e.g., stress → preventive rest → stability). Changing too much at once fails, but changing one small thing at a time allows the brain to learn.

### 7. Mindfulness & ACT Elements
- **Awareness is the starting point for change**
- **Accept, don't fight:** "manage" triggers, not "eliminate" them
- Stage 1: the whole process cultivates awareness of triggers
- **Why it helps:** Chronic illness patients often fall into an anxious loop of "I must cure myself," which is itself a stressor. When you can accept "I have triggers, and that's normal," you free up energy to learn effective coping. Awareness transforms you from "passive victim" to "active observer" — that identity shift itself is healing.

## Change Mechanisms

1. **Safety → Exploration → Insight → Action** (Polyvagal)
2. **User-led discovery, not expert prescriptions** (MI + Trauma-Informed)
3. **Specific, small, personalized strategies** (Mind-Body + Neuroplasticity)
4. **Acceptance + Awareness attitude** (ACT + Mindfulness)

## Integration: Why This Design Works

When all these elements combine, the result is not just "knowing a trigger" — it is experiencing a process of regaining agency:

- **Safety → Exploration → Insight → Action sequence** (Polyvagal Theory): ensures the nervous system is regulated before diving into difficult content
- **User-led discovery** (MI + Trauma-Informed): you find your own answers rather than receiving prescriptions
- **Specific, small, personalized strategies** (Mind-Body + Neuroplasticity): strategies that grow from your real life context, not generic advice
- **Acceptance + Awareness** (ACT + Mindfulness): freeing energy from fighting symptoms toward learning effective coping

You learn how to observe yourself, understand yourself, treat yourself gently, and find coping methods that belong to you. That process itself is healing.

## ME/CFS & Long COVID Note

This journal is particularly relevant for ME/CFS, Long COVID, and similar chronic conditions. These conditions are highly individualized with no one-size-fits-all solution. The guided process helps you become the expert on your own body, discovering your unique triggers and building personalized strategies — rather than following generic protocols that may not fit your situation.

## Output Example

**Today's Exploration Summary**

**Identified Trigger:** Working 10+ hours for two consecutive days + sleeping less than 6 hours, leading to severe fatigue and brain fog on the third day.

**Your Insight:** You noticed that you often sacrifice sleep to "catch up on progress," but this actually makes the next day less efficient, creating a vicious cycle.

**Existing Strategies:** You have been trying to set work-hour limits, but it's hard to stick to when under pressure.

**New Coping Strategies:**
1. Preventive rest: rest proactively when you still feel "okay," rather than waiting until you crash
2. Sleep-first principle: set a non-negotiable bedtime (e.g., 11 PM)
3. Energy tracking: simply rate your energy 1-10 each day to catch warning signs earlier

**Confidence Levels:**
- Sleep-first principle: 7/10
- Preventive rest: 5/10 (worried it will affect progress)

**Next Step:** Try the "sleep-first principle" this week and see if it reduces trigger frequency.

# Knowing Your Needs（認識你的需求）

A guided self-exploration exercise in Rosebud that translates emotions into needs, and needs into next steps.

## Overview

Leads users through a systematic process: starting from emotions and body sensations, moving through energy assessment, need identification, and ending with an actionable plan. One question at a time, following the user's pace, with options provided to help precise expression.

## 7-Step Process

1. **Identify current emotions** — start broad, then refine (e.g. "anxiety" → "fear of losing control")
2. **Body awareness scan** — notice sensations (tension, heaviness, warmth) and connect to emotions
3. **Assess capacity across 4 domains** — emotional, physical, mental, social energy levels (0-10)
4. **Identify needs** — based on the above, surface what's truly needed (rest? connection? sense of control?)
5. **Categorize needs** — which can you fulfill yourself vs. which require others' help?
6. **Create action plan** — translate needs into concrete, executable steps
7. **Reflection & emotional re-check** — integrate insights and confirm state change

## Theoretical Foundations

### 1. Nonviolent Communication (NVC)
- **Observation vs. evaluation separation:** describe facts first, then feelings — avoid jumping to blame
- **Feeling → need connection:** every emotion has an unmet need behind it (e.g. "anger" may mean "need for respect")
- **Request vs. demand:** turn needs into specific, negotiable requests rather than commands

### 2. Emotion-Focused Therapy (EFT)
- **Emotions as signals:** emotions are navigation systems telling you where attention is needed
- **Primary vs. secondary emotions:** surface anger (secondary) may mask deeper hurt or fear (primary)
- **Body wisdom:** emotions manifest as body sensations — scanning the body helps identify emotions accurately

### 3. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
- **Thought → emotion → behavior chain:** your interpretation of a situation (not the situation itself) determines your emotional response
- **Automatic thought identification:** many needs are ignored due to beliefs like "I shouldn't have this need"
- **Evidence testing:** challenge the inner critic with "Is this need really unreasonable?"

### 4. Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT)
- **Control circle vs. influence circle:** distinguish "needs I can directly meet" vs. "needs requiring others' cooperation"
- **Values-directed action:** even if a need can't be met immediately, take small steps toward your values
- **Creative hopelessness:** accepting "some needs can't be met right now" frees energy for what you can do

### 5. Self-Determination Theory (SDT)
- **Three basic needs:** autonomy (I can choose), competence (I can do it), relatedness (I am seen)
- **Intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation:** meeting basic psychological needs fuels intrinsic motivation
- **Need interaction:** when basic needs are met, people have capacity for higher-level needs

### 6. Solution-Focused Brief Therapy (SFBT)
- **Exception inquiry:** "Was there a time this need was met? What did you do then?" — find replicable successes
- **Miracle question:** "If you woke up tomorrow with this need fulfilled, what would be different?" — concretize goals
- **Scaling question:** "On 0-10, how satisfied are you now? What would it take to go up one point?" — decompose action

### 7. Motivational Interviewing (MI)
- **Elicit change talk:** through questions, let the user voice "I want / I need" rather than being told
- **Rolling reflection:** restate and slightly deepen what the user said, helping them hear their own needs
- **Resistance is normal:** when the user says "but…", explore the obstacle with curiosity rather than opposition

## Theory → Conversation Flow Mapping

### Stage 1: Identify Current Emotions (EFT + NVC)
- "What emotion are you feeling right now?"
- "Where in your body is this emotion most noticeable?"
- "If this feeling had a color/temperature/texture, what would it be?"
- Provide emotion wheel with sub-emotions
- "Besides this emotion, are there other feelings present at the same time?"

### Stage 2: Body Awareness (EFT + Somatic Psychology)
- "Would you like to scan head-to-toe, or focus on a specific area?"
- "Is the sensation in that area tight, heavy, tingling, or something else?"

### Stage 3: Assess 4-Domain Capacity (SDT + ACT)
- Emotional / physical / mental / social capacity rated 0-10

### Stage 4: Derive Needs from Emotions & Capacity (NVC + SDT)
- "Based on what you shared, I'm guessing you might need: [3-5 needs]. Which resonate?"

### Stage 5: Controllable vs. Needs Requiring Help (ACT + SFBT)
- "Which can you fulfill directly? Which need others' cooperation?"
- "Have you successfully met a similar need before? What did you do?"

### Stage 6: Action Plan (SFBT + CBT + MI)
- "What would it take to go up one point?"
- "What obstacles might you encounter? What's Plan B?"
- "Does this plan feel doable? Where does it need adjustment?"

## Why This Design Works — Change Mechanisms

- **Emotion labeling** — naming reduces emotional intensity
- **Body awareness** — grounds abstract feelings in concrete sensation
- **Capacity normalization** — legitimizes having limits
- **Choice architecture** — providing options reduces decision paralysis
- **Control circle** — reduces helplessness by clarifying what's actionable
- **Behavioral activation** — small steps build momentum
- **If-Then implementation intentions** — pre-planned responses increase follow-through
- **Meta-awareness** — noticing your own patterns creates space for change

## Scope & Boundaries

**Suitable for:**
- Everyday emotional difficulties
- Unclear needs
- Decision paralysis
- Preparing for interpersonal communication

**Not suitable for:**
- Acute crisis
- Severe psychiatric symptoms
- Complex trauma
- Relationship violence

**High-risk protocol:** Provide Taiwan resources (1925, 1995, 113) and recommend professional help
